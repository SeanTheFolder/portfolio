Methodology

How I Run an Engagement

A repeatable five-phase process I use across consulting engagements and full-time roles. Adapted to scope, but always anchored in the same principles: assess first, prioritize ruthlessly, communicate up, execute with precision.

01

Listen and Assess (Days 1 to 30)

No changes. Understand what the business actually does, who the real decision-makers are, what the risk posture looks like on paper versus in reality, and where the regulatory exposure sits. Inventory systems, controls, vendors, and dependencies. Talk to the people who actually run the workflows.

02

Frame the Risk in Business Terms (Days 30 to 45)

Produce a written risk assessment and a prioritized remediation roadmap, framed in operational continuity, regulatory exposure, and reputational cost. Translate technical findings into funded decisions. Get executive alignment before building.

03

Build the Crosswalk and Architecture (Days 45 to 75)

Map every required control to a regulatory citation, business-impact rationale, and technical or procedural artifact. The crosswalk is the document auditors and leadership both find useful. The architecture decisions made here compound for years.

04

Execute With Precision (Days 75 to 180)

Sequence implementation by risk reduction per dollar. Inventory and access management before advanced detection. Operational basics before exotic tooling. Every control gets paired with monitoring, documentation, and a runbook the operating team can actually use.

05

Govern and Iterate (Ongoing)

Monthly board-level posture dashboard. Quarterly assessment against the crosswalk. Annual third-party validation. Tune from real signal, not theoretical models. The program improves continuously because the feedback loop is wired in from day one, not bolted on afterward.

For Boards & Executive Leadership

Security That a Board Can Actually Govern

The hardest part of executive security is not the controls. It is the translation. A board cannot govern what it cannot understand, and a CISO who leads with CVEs loses the room within ninety seconds.

I report security risk in the same three terms a board already uses to make decisions: operational continuity, regulatory exposure, and reputational cost. A critical vulnerability in a patient-facing system is not a CVSS 9.8. It is a potential breach notification event, a six-figure remediation cost, and an OCR investigation. When the board understands inaction in those terms, funding decisions get easier and the organization stops debating whether to invest in security and starts debating how much.

Every quarter the board sees the same one-page dashboard: top risks ranked by business impact, posture trend over time, regulatory deadlines on the horizon, and the dollar value of incidents prevented or contained. Reports are short on purpose. The board should be making decisions, not reading reports.

FAQ

Quick answers to the questions hiring managers, recruiters, board members, and consulting prospects ask most.

What engagement models are available?+

Six options: full-time CISO or IT Director, fractional CISO (vCISO) at 1 to 3 days a week, project-based consulting (HITRUST readiness, security program builds, Sentinel deployments, AI governance design), board advisory on a quarterly retainer, interim or transition CISO during M&A or between hires, and one-time assessments including HIPAA gap analysis, M&A security due diligence, and penetration testing. See the Engagement Models section above for full detail.

What does the healthcare compliance landscape actually require?+

For home health and hospice: HIPAA Security and Privacy Rules as the baseline, HITECH for breach notification, OIG compliance program guidance for both home health agencies and hospices, 42 CFR Part 418 Medicare Conditions of Participation for clinical record-keeping, the 42 CFR Part 2 final rule for SUD records (active enforcement since February 2026), the Information Blocking Rule under the 21st Century Cures Act, and False Claims Act exposure on billing systems. HITRUST CSF is the industry framework that ties it together. The compliance program at Harbors is built toward HITRUST.

How is AI governance handled in HIPAA-regulated environments?+

AI governance is data governance with extra steps. Sanctioned AI tools (Microsoft Copilot Studio, Security Copilot, Anthropic Claude, Claude Code with MCP servers, plus Gemini and Antigravity for evaluation) operate under Purview DLP scoped to AI prompt surfaces, Conditional Access on agent endpoints, and full audit logging through Sentinel. A multi-LLM evaluation framework drives per-task model selection rather than vendor lock-in. Shadow AI is treated as a DLP problem, not a policy conversation: block what is not approved and fast-track approval for legitimate use cases.

How is security risk reported to a board?+

Every significant risk gets translated into one of three terms the board already uses to make decisions: operational continuity, regulatory exposure, or reputational cost. A critical vulnerability in a patient-facing system is not a CVSS 9.8. It is a potential breach notification event, a six-figure remediation cost, and an OCR investigation. Reporting cadence is a standing one-page quarterly dashboard. Top risks mapped to business impact, posture trend over time, regulatory deadlines on the horizon, dollar value of incidents prevented or contained. The board should be making decisions, not reading reports.

What does the first 90 days of a new engagement look like?+

Days 1 to 30 is listening and assessment. No changes. Inventory systems, controls, vendors, and dependencies. Days 30 to 60 is producing a written risk assessment and a prioritized remediation roadmap framed in business terms. Get executive alignment before building. Days 60 to 90 is execution on the highest-priority items with visible, measurable outcomes. By day 90 the organization has at least one tangible security improvement it did not have before, and leadership has a clear picture of where the program is going and why. See the Methodology section for the full five-phase process.
