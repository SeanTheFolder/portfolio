Essay · AI Governance

AI Governance Is Data Governance With Extra Steps

Most AI governance debates skip the unglamorous part: deciding what data the model can see, what prompts it can take, and where its outputs can land. Build that boundary in Purview and Conditional Access first, then build the agent against it. The agent layer is the interface. The boundary is the governance.

Essay · Board Practice

The One-Page Risk Dashboard Rule

The board cannot govern what it cannot understand. Every significant risk gets translated into one of three terms the board already uses: operational continuity, regulatory exposure, reputational cost. The dashboard is one page. The trend is over time. The reading time is under three minutes. Anything else is wasted.

Essay · Multi-LLM Strategy

Why Vendor Lock-In on LLMs Is the Strategic Mistake of 2026

The capability differential between Claude, Gemini, GPT, Antigravity, and Copilot is large enough that picking the right model per task produces materially better outcomes. The operational cost of supporting multiple approved models is low if your governance layer is built right.

Hiring the First Security Engineer at a Healthcare Nonprofit

The first security hire is the one that defines the next five. The job description should match the next twenty-four months of work, not a generic CISSP checklist. The interview should test for translation between technical risk and business language. The first ninety days should be spent measuring posture, not buying tools. Notes from running this hire twice.

The Five Questions That Decide Whether a Healthcare Acquisition Is a Cyber Liability

A working framework from M&A security due diligence engagements. Identity hygiene, EHR integration debt, undocumented PHI flows, vendor sprawl, and incident history. The diligence memo a healthcare acquirer should be reading before the LOI is signed, written for the board member who has to vote on the deal.

Essay · IT Director Practice

Why the IT Director and the CISO Should Sometimes Be the Same Person

For mid-market healthcare and nonprofit organizations, splitting IT and security across two leaders is often the wrong structural choice. The split creates handoff cost, duplicated tooling, and a permanent priority argument. A field case for the unified role at the right organizational scale, and the size at which the split finally makes sense.

Field Notes · Vendor Risk

The BAA Is Not the Control: A Practical Vendor Risk Program for Healthcare

Most vendor risk programs collect a signed Business Associate Agreement and stop. The BAA is the legal instrument. The control is what you actually do to inspect, segment, and decommission third-party access. A field guide to running vendor risk on a small team, with the questions that matter and the questionnaires that do not.
